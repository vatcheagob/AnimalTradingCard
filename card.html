Honey Badger

In the gland at the base of it's tail, the honey badger stores a liquid just as powerful as a skunk's.

  • Scientific name: Mellivora capensis
  • Type: Mammals
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Weight: 13 to 30 lbs

Some say honey badgers don't care, but that reputation is hardly fair. In fact, these medium-size members of the weasal family have super-sized atittutes. They can be found thoruhgout Africa, Saurdi Arabia, Iran and western Asia.
